Son-in-Law Eggs

Description

This is an amazing Thai dish, consisting of fried eggs, fried shallots, garlic chips, and a sweet sauce. A real treat if you ask me.

Ingredients

  • 1 Tbsp Finely Diced Shallot

  • 2 Tbsp Tamarind Juice

  • 3 Tbsp Chopped and Finely Packed Palm Sugar

  • 1 Tbsp Water

  • 1 Tbsp & 1 tsp Fish Sauce

  • 4 Boiled Duck Eggs or 5 Chicken Eggs, Peeled and Cooled

  • Fried Shallots, or 2 Heads of Shallot(small)

    • Skip the "Fried Shallots" Steps if You Bought Fried Shallots

  • 3-4 Cloves Garlic for Frying

  • Dried Chillies Optional

  • Cilantro Leaves

Steps

Sauce

  1. In a small pot, saute the diced shallots in a little oil until the shallots have browned slightly.

  2. Add palm sugar, tamarind juice, water and fish sauce to the shallots and let it simmer until the sugar is dissolved and the sauce has thickened slightly ( about 3 to 5 minutes).

    • You can crush the sugar lumps with a wooden spoon while it is simmering to help the sugar dissolve more quickly.

  3. Taste the sauce and adjust seasoning as needed.

    • Note:The sauce will be mellowed out by the eggs.

Fried Shallots

  1. Slice the shallots thinly lengthwise, and put them on a plate lined with a paper towel.

  2. Sprinkle salt over the shallots and let them sit for 10 minutes.

  3. After letting the shallots sit, press a paper towel onto the shallots to absorb all the liquid that has been drawn out by the salt.

  4. Heat just enough oil in a wok or shallow pan to cover the shallots. Turn the heat up to medium high and immediately add the shallots.

  5. When the shallots are bubbling, turn the heat down to low and keep them bubbling until they are golden brown and cripsy.

    • Note: They burn quickly, so watch them!

  6. Remove the shallots with a skimmer and drain on a paper towel and turn off the heat.

Garlic Chips

  1. Slice the Garlic Cloves Thinly

  2. In the same oil used for the fried shallots, add the sliced garlic and THEN turn the heat back on to low.

  3. Keep frying the garlic on low until the garlic has turned a light golden color and it stops bubbling.

  4. Remove the garlic chips and drain on a paper towel. turn off the heat.

Fried Chillies

Optional

  1. Fry the dried chilies on low heat until they turn a maroon color and smell smoky.

  2. Remove and drain on a paper towel

Fried Eggs

  1. Add enough oil to a pot so that it comes halfway up the eggs. Heat the oil on medium high until the oil reaches 375-400°F

  2. While the oil is heating up, dry the eggs with a paper towel.

    • This will keep the oil from splattering too much

  3. Add the eggs to the oil, one at a time, and fry them until they become golden brown. Flip the eggs and brown the other side.

  4. Remove the eggs from the oil and drain on a paper towel.

To Finish

  1. Cut the eggs in half with a sharp knife and arrange them on a plate, cut side up.

  2. Reheat the sauce, adding water if it has become too thick, and then drizzle the sauce over the eggs.

  3. Top with fried shallots, garlic, chilies, cilantro and serve with rice!
