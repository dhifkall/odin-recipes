Son-in-Law Eggs

Description

A Thai dish that is great for breakfast! Fried eggs, with a delicious
sweet sauce and some tasty fried garlic chips and fried shallots- delicious!

Ingredients

  • 1 Tablespoon Finely Diced Shallot
  • 2 Tablespoons Tamarind Juice
  • 3 Tablespoons Chopped and Finely Packed Palm Sugar
  • 1 Tablespoon Water
  • 1 Tablespoon Plus 1 Teaspoon Fish Sauce
  • 4 Boiled Duck Eggs or 5 Chicken Eggs, Peeled and Cooled
  • Fried Shallots, or 2 Heads of Shallot(small)
    • Skip the "Fried Shallots" Steps if You Bought Fried Shallots
  • 3-4 Cloves Garlic for Frying
  • Optional Dried Chillies
  • Cilantro Leaves

Steps

Sauce

  1. In a small pot, saute the diced shallots in a little oil until
    the shallots are browned slightly.

  2. Add palm sugar, tamarind juice, water and fish sauce to the shallots
    and let it simmer until the sugar is dissolved and the sauce
    has thickened slightly ( about 3 to 5 minutes).
    • You can crush the sugar lumps while it is simmering
      to help the sugar dissolve more quickly.

  3. Taste the sauce and adjust seasoning as needed
    • The sauce will be mellowed out by the eggs

Fried Shallots

  1. Slice Thinly the 2 Heads of Shallots Lengthwise, and
    Put Them on a Plate Lined with a Paper Towel

  2. Sprinkle Salt over the Shallots and Let Them Sit for 10 minutes.

  3. After Letting the Shallots Sit, Press a Paper Towel
    onto the Shallots to Absorb All the Liquid

  4. Heat Just Enough Oil in a Wok or Shallow Pan to Cover the Shallots.
    Turn up the Heat to Medium High
    and Immediately Add the Shallots

  5. When the Shallots are Bubbling, Turn the Heat Down to Low
    and Keep Them Bubbling until they are Golden
    Brown and Cripsy
    • They Burn Quickly, so Watch Them!

  6. Remove the Shallots with a Skimmer and Drain on a Paper
    Towel and Turn off the Heat

Garlic Chips

  1. Slice the Garlic Cloves Thinly

  2. In the Same Oil Used for the Fried Shallots, Add the
    Sliced Garlic and Then Turn the Heat
    Back on to Low.

  3. Keep Frying the Garlic on Low Until the Garlic has Turned
    a Light Golden Color and it Stops Bubbling.

  4. Remove the Garlic Chips and Drain on a Paper Towel.
    Turn off the Heat.
